You can’t just find a pattern and copy it into your program the way you can with off-the-shelf functions or libraries.  
A design pattern is not a specific piece of code, but a general concept for solving a particular problem.  

You can follow the pattern’s guidelines and implement a solution that fits the realities of your own program.

# Classification of Design Patterns


---

## 1. Creational Patterns
Creational patterns provide object creation mechanisms that:

- Increase flexibility  
- Promote code reuse  
- Decouple object creation from object usage  

These patterns help control how objects are created. creates a system as to where and how the objects are created.

---

## 2. Structural Patterns
Structural patterns explain how to:

- Assemble objects and classes into larger structures  
- Keep these structures flexible  
- Maintain efficiency  

They focus on the composition of classes and objects. assemble classes and the structure into a larger structure to make it easy to join, expand upon and further. 

---

## 3. Behavioral Patterns
Behavioral patterns focus on:

- Effective communication between objects  
- Proper assignment of responsibilities  
- Clear interaction patterns  

They define how objects collaborate and share responsibilities. Defines how objects collaborate and share responsiblity to which makes the sense.
